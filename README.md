# NoBSC: Notears-Based Smooth Constraint
Directed acyclic graph (DAG) structure learning aims to recover causal relationships among observed variables by representing them as a directed graph without cycles. A key limitation of many existing methods is their inability to incorporate domain knowledge, particularly forbidden-edge constraints, which specify certain edges should not exist. Ignoring such constraints can lead to spurious causal relationships in the learned graph. Recent approaches attempt to integrate prior knowledge through constrained continuous optimization by explicitly excluding forbidden edges during estimation. However, these formulations often involve non-smooth constraint functions, which weaken theoretical guarantees for gradient-based optimization and may result in unstable or inconsistent solutions. To address these issues, we propose a structural learning algorithm that directly incorporates gradient information for forbidden-edge constraints. This reformulation preserves the differentiability of the structural constraint function, thereby enhancing the numerical stability of gradient-based optimization while  softly enforcing domain-specific exclusions with data-driven learning rather than imposing hard constraints. Experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate that the proposed method effectively eliminates forbidden edges, maintains appropriate sparsity, and achieves more accurate recovery of the underlying causal structure compared to existing benchmark approaches.
